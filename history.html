Our Church History

Celebrating over 220 years of faith, fellowship, and service in the Malden community

Timeline of Our Journey

1803

Church Founded

First Baptist Church of Malden was established on December 27, 1803. The first worship service was held in Faulkner Barn, and the first Lord's Supper was celebrated in January 1804.

1804

First Meeting House

The First Meeting House was built and dedicated in September 1804 on Salem Street, providing the congregation with their first dedicated place of worship.

1820

Church Incorporated

The church was officially incorporated with the power to hold property up to $20,000, establishing its legal foundation for future growth.

1827

First Choir

The first mention of a choir appears in church records, marking the beginning of our musical ministry tradition.

1843

Second House of Worship

The Second House of Worship was completed after a building committee was formed in 1840 to plan for the growing congregation's needs.

1855 & 1862

Devastating Fires

Two devastating fires destroyed church buildings in 1855 and 1862, testing the faith and resilience of the congregation but strengthening their resolve to rebuild.

1864

Fourth House of Worship

Following the fires, the Fourth House of Worship was built, demonstrating the congregation's commitment to maintaining their place of worship.

1891

Fifth House of Worship

The Fifth House of Worship was dedicated at a cost of $96,000, representing a significant investment in the church's future and the community's faith.

1945-1969

Rev. Hillyer Straton

Rev. Hillyer Straton served as the longest-serving pastor in church history, providing spiritual leadership for 24 years during a period of significant growth and community outreach.

1997

First Female Senior Pastor

Rev. Martha Dominy was installed as the first female Senior Pastor, marking a historic milestone in the church's leadership and commitment to inclusive ministry.

2003

200th Anniversary

The church celebrated its 200th anniversary, reflecting on two centuries of faithful service to the Malden community and looking forward to future ministries.
